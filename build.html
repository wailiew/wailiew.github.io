My First Build

Few years back, my brother gave me his an old gaming rig. I tried to replace some parts to make it work a little better. After a while, I felt like the system is not strong enough to handle the games I was playing, so after hours and hours of watching Youtube video ranging from Jayztwocents, LinusTechTips, Bitwit, and Paul's Hardware. I decided that I am comfortable enough to build my own computer. I chose Fractal Design Define R6 is a great start due to its great airflow, I can open the front panel and remove the hard drive cages if I need to. I also chose not to put any RGB fans on my case and install a few RGB strips. After a few months I decided to replace the Evga CLC 280mm AIO to a Nzxt Kraken X62 due to its beautiful Pump. I also ran some tests on my case and concluded that placing the radiator on the front of the case lower the CPU temperature about 5 to 8 percent.

Building My friend's PC

After I felt comfortable enough, I offered my friend to build him his own gaming PC. He had a budget of $1000 so I chose some parts carefully making sure to not go over budget. He has two dogs so I chose Nzxt H500 for its small footprint therefore he can put his computer on the desk in order to avoid dust build-up. Even though he doesn't care about the appearance of the PC, I still want to find something that looks decent. While trying to keep the cose low, I make the decision to purchase an used Evga GeForce GTX 1070 Ti SC from ebay and repaste the graphics card. I also donated a Evga CLC 280mm that I have laying around.

The funnest build that I did.

This has to be the funnest build I had ever done. My friend has a pretty high end computer already at the time and he asked me to help him make it look better. He has different colors of RGB coming from different component therefore it looks like unicorn vomit. In this case, there isn't really a lot of component change. CPU, RAM, GPU stayed the same but we changed his AIO from a Corsair H100i RGB Platinum to a Nzxt Kraken X72 and added a white sleeve on the tube to match the case. The biggest change to the PC is the chassis from Corsair Crystal 570X to Nzxt H700I. We also implemented push/pull configuration to the AIO to make the airflow better. The photo doesn't show it but the case is white so the blue/purple hue coming from inside the chassis really makes the computer stand out.
